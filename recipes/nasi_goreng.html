Nasi Goreng

Fried Rice

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp (10mL) vegetable oil
  • 1 lb (450 g) sliced chicken breast, sliced sirloin, shelled prawns or cubed firm tofu
  • 1 cup (250 mL) 1/2 inch (1cm) chopped celery
  • 1 cup (250 mL) medium-diced red onion
  • 1 cup (250 mL) medium-diced red pepper

  • 1/2 cup (125 mL) pineapple wedges
  • ½ cup (125 mL) cored apple, 1/16-inch (2 mm) slices lengthwise
  • ¼ cup (60 mL) currants
  • ¼ cup (60 mL) coarsely chopped cilantro
  • ¼ cup (60 mL) plain yogurt

    • Stir-fry sauce (recipe follows)

  • ¼ cup (60 mL) soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp (15 mL) brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp (15 mL) minced ginger
  • 1 tbsp (15 mL) minced garlic
  • 1 tbsp (15 mL) rice vinegar
  • 2 tsp (10 mL) sesame oil

    • Cooked rice (recipe follows)

  • 1½ cups (375 mL) shredded green cabbage (tightly packed)
  • ¾ cup (180 mL) sushi rice or jasmine rice
  • ¾ cup (180 mL) converted long-grain rice
  • ⅓ cup (80 mL) mango chutney
  • 1 tbsp (15 mL) sambal oelek (a chili and garlic paste found in the Asian food section of your grocery store)
  • 1 tbsp (15 mL) curry powder
  • 1 tsp (5 mL) salt
  • 2 cups minus 1 tbsp (485 mL) water

Steps

COOKED RICE

Rice cooker method

  1. Place all ingredients in a rice cooker insert with a minimum capacity of 6 cups (1.5 L).
  2. Mix all the ingredients well so that all the sauces and spices are evenly distributed.
  3. Cover the rice cooker and start the cook cycle. Once the cycle is finished, allow the rice to rest for 20 minutes to complete cooking. This allows the grains of rice to set properly and prevents gummy rice.
  4. Remove the rice from the cooker and spread it onto a clean baking sheet. Allow to cool to room temperature before proceeding to the rest of the recipe. If preparing ahead, transfer to a sealed container and refrigerate overnight. STIR-FRY SAUCE 1. Mix all the ingredients together. Can be prepared and stored refrigerated up to 4 days ahead.

NASI GORENG

  1. Heat a nonstick skillet or wok over medium heat. Pour in vegetable oil and allow to heat until it ripples. 2. If adding protein (chicken, beef, prawns or tofu), sauté in the hot oil until almost cooked through.
  2. Add celery, red onion and red pepper, cooking for 2 minutes until vegetables are slightly tender.
  3. Toss in the rice and stir well, evenly distributing all ingredients.
  4. Drizzle in the stir-fry sauce and continue tossing, coating each grain of rice with the sauce until the rice is thoroughly heated. Divide into bowls.
  5. Garnish each bowl evenly with pineapple, apple, currants and cilantro.
  6. Dollop 1 tablespoon (15 mL) of yogurt over each bowl and serve immediately.
